Wanderlust

International Travel

My experiences are currently isolated to Mexico, the Caribbean, and Costa Rica.

Puerto Vallarta, Mexico

A very energetic and alive port city. The lanscape allows the best of any leisure activities. Hiking, ziplining, snorking, and so much more.

Ocho Rios, Jamaica

I spent one week in this tropical paradise for my friends Paul and Emily's wedding. It was hard to leave the endless supply of booze and gourmet cuisine.

Costa Rica

This was a trip of a life time. Three close friends and myself rented a car and traversed the country for a 10 day adventure. We started our trip trekking up to Monteverde, where we explored the sky bridges and trails of the cloud forest. We then jumped to La Fortuna, endulging in ziplines and lake Arenal. Unfortnately, we encountered a down pour on the next leg of our travels, that washed the roads out perventing us from exploring the Irazu Volcano. So, we moved on to the Caribbean coastal town of Puerto Viejo. This coastal town is a top destination for Ex-Pats.

Domestic Travel

I have currently visited the following states; CA, OR, AZ, NV, NY, LA, IL, NY, KY, TN, TX, CO, FL, NC, GA, MO. Listed below are five of my favorite places I have visited

Columbia River Gorge, OR

I spent four days hiking multiple trails in the Columbia River Gorge. It was one of the most majestic places I have ever seen

Yosemite National Park, CA

I spent only one day here when I was fourteen, but layed the foundation for my desire to travel.

New Orleans, LA

I have visited NOLA multiple times, and each time it steals a little more of my soul. The grittiness and pulse of the city are so seductive.

Atlanta, GA

The ATL, another city I have made repeat visits. My brother from another mother lives in Atlanta, and is the main reason I keep going back.

Sedona, AZ

Very few places emit an energy like Sedona does. It's difficult to describe. There is a force that naturally energizes your soul. The Red rock, the lush greenry, and endless sky create an amaing sight.
